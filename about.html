About ConTexture

The story behind the app.

The developer

Timothy Frey is a US expat living in the Netherlands, working in maritime logistics by day and pursuing writing as a personal passion. ConTexture grew out of the need for a better way to organise long-form creative work.


Based in the Netherlands 🇳🇱
Apple Developer since 2024

Why ConTexture?

Writing has always been something I've done for myself — short stories, long-form fiction, worldbuilding, notes that grow into something larger. It's a hobby and a passion, not a profession, and that's exactly what makes it worth taking seriously as a creative practice.

The problem I kept running into was organisation. Long-form writing has a lot of moving parts — characters, locations, timelines, research, maps, chapters that reference all of it. I was spreading everything across multiple apps and losing the thread every time I switched between them.

I wanted one app where a chapter could sit next to its characters, where typing [[ would link to a worldbuilding note, where @ would pull in a map reference, and where my iPad, iPhone, and Mac all showed the same thing. I couldn't find exactly what I needed, so I built it.

ConTexture is named for the way writing lives in context — a chapter means more when you can see the character sheet beside it, a scene lands differently when the map is one tap away. The name is also a deliberate portmanteau: context and texture. Good writing has both.

What's next

ConTexture 1.0 is the foundation. On the roadmap: real-time collaboration improvements, a community forum, timeline view, and improved import compatibility. If there's something you need, the best way to influence what gets built is to get in touch or visit the community page.
