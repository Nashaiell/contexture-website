About ConTexture

Built by a writer, for writers.

The developer

Timothy Frey is a US expat living in the Netherlands, working in maritime logistics by day and writing fantasy fiction by night. ConTexture grew out of the frustration of managing a novel across too many apps.


Based in the Netherlands 🇳🇱
Apple Developer since 2024

Why ConTexture?

I write long-form fiction. It has constructed languages, dozens of characters, hand-drawn maps, and chapters that need to reference all of it constantly. I kept splitting my workflow between Scrivener, Obsidian, Bear, and the Notes app — and losing the thread every time I switched.

I wanted one app where a chapter could sit next to its characters, where typing [[ would link to a worldbuilding note, where @ would pull in a map reference, and where my iPad, iPhone, and Mac all had exactly the same thing in front of them. I couldn't find it, so I built it.

ConTexture is named for the way writing lives in context — a chapter means more when you can see the character sheet beside it, a scene lands differently when the map is one tap away. The name is also a deliberate portmanteau: context and texture. Good writing has both.

What's next

ConTexture 1.0 is the foundation. On the roadmap: real-time collaboration improvements, a community forum, timeline view, and deeper Scrivener import compatibility. If there's something you need, the best way to influence what gets built is to get in touch or visit the community page.
